## 1. Google Business Profile (GBP) — highest local-pack lever

The local 3-pack drives the majority of clicks for "[city] personal injury lawyer" searches, and **primary category is the #1 local ranking factor**. In 2026, Google rewards *actively managed* profiles and demotes "set-and-forget" ones.

**One profile per physical office** (Roseville HQ + each real office — Sacramento, Oakland, Stockton, Modesto, Fairfield, Chico, Redding). Do **not** create GBP listings for service-area-only cities (Alameda, Elk Grove, etc.) — fake locations get suspended.

**Per-profile setup checklist:**
- [ ] **Primary category:** "Personal injury attorney" (exactly). Add secondary categories that apply: "Trial attorney," "Legal services," "Law firm."
- [ ] NAP matches the source of truth byte-for-byte.
- [ ] Service areas listed (the cities each office covers).
- [ ] Hours set and accurate (you advertise 24/7 phone — reflect that in the description, not fake office hours).
- [ ] 10+ photos: office exterior/interior, team, signage. Add new photos monthly.
- [ ] Services list populated with your practice areas (car accident, truck accident, wrongful death, etc.).
- [ ] Description: natural, mentions practice areas + city + "$1 billion+ recovered."
- [ ] Link each profile to the **matching city landing page** (e.g., Oakland GBP → /oakland), not just the homepage.

**Ongoing (the part that actually moves rank):**
- [ ] **Post weekly** via Google Posts (case results, FAQs, local safety tips). Reuse blog/accident-news content.
- [ ] **Respond to every review within 48 hours** (see §3).
- [ ] Answer GBP Q&A; seed it with your real FAQs.
- [ ] Keep hours updated around holidays.

## 2. NAP consistency — the silent ranking killer

Google cross-references your Name/Address/Phone across the web to confirm you're a real, single business. **Inconsistencies — even "St." vs "Street," or an old phone number — can cut local rank 40%+** and confuse AI engines about your identity.

**Action:**
- [ ] Lock the source of truth above. Decide the canonical address format once (abbreviations, suite format) and never vary it.
- [ ] **Audit every existing listing** (use a tool like BrightLocal/Whitespark, or audit manually) and fix mismatches: GBP, Yelp, Justia, Avvo, FindLaw, Super Lawyers, Martindale, BBB, Chamber of Commerce, social profiles.

- [ ] When anything changes (new suite, number), update GBP first, then propagate everywhere the same week.

## 3. Reviews — volume, velocity, and recency

Reviews are ~16% of local-pack weight, power the star ratings users click, and feed AI-engine trust. Google weighs **steady, recent** reviews over a big one-time burst.

**Action:**
- [ ] Build a **post-resolution review request** into case closing: every satisfied client gets a direct GBP review link by text + email within 48 hours of settlement.
- [ ] Aim for a steady cadence (e.g., 8–15 new Google reviews/month across offices) rather than spikes.
- [ ] **Respond to all reviews** — thank positives by name; respond to negatives calmly and professionally (never disclose case details — bar/ethics).

- [ ] **On-site:** once you have a real aggregate (count + average), we add `AggregateRating` schema so stars can show in search. *I need the live numbers from you to implement this — I will not invent them (fake review schema violates Google policy and is a real liability for a law firm).*

## 5. Link building (realistic for Authority Score 23)

At your authority level, **local relevance beats raw volume**, and *earned* links beat purchased ones. Skip directory-link spam and PBNs (penalty risk, wasted budget).

**Highest-ROI tactics:**
- [ ] **Local sponsorships** — youth sports, schools, community events, charity 5Ks. These earn legitimate `.org`/local-news backlinks and goodwill. (Frank already supports youth court programs — get those links.)
- [ ] **Local news / PR** — pitch notable case wins, community involvement, and expert commentary to Sacramento Business Journal, local TV/news sites. Frank's 2023 Consumer Attorney of the Year award is link-worthy.
- [ ] **Original data / "linkbait"** — publish an annual study (e.g., "Most Dangerous Intersections in Sacramento County," "California Uninsured-Driver Report") and pitch it to journalists. Data assets earn the most links. This pairs with the planned dangerous-highway pages.
- [ ] **Bar association & alma mater** — CLE talks, committee service, guest posts (Gonzaga Law, BYU alumni networks).
- [ ] **Medical/clinic partnerships** — reciprocal referral relationships with local clinics, chiropractors, PT centers can yield links and case referrals.
- [ ] **Scholarship** — a small annual scholarship hosted on a `/scholarship` page earns `.edu` links when posted to university financial-aid pages.

**Avoid:** paid link networks, low-quality directory blasts, link exchanges with unrelated sites.
