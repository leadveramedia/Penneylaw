Understanding Your Rights

Who Can File a Wrongful Death Claim

California law specifies who is eligible to file a wrongful death lawsuit on behalf of a deceased loved one.

Eligible Family Members

  • Surviving Spouse or Domestic Partner

    The deceased's legal spouse or registered domestic partner has primary standing to file.

  • Children

    Both biological and legally adopted children may file a wrongful death claim.

  • Grandchildren

    If the deceased's children are also deceased, grandchildren may be eligible.

  • Other Dependents

    Those who were financially dependent on the deceased may have grounds to file.

Compensation You May Recover

  • Funeral and burial expenses
  • Medical bills incurred before death
  • Loss of future income and benefits
  • Loss of companionship and support
  • Loss of household services
  • Pain and suffering of survivors

Protecting Your Family

Why You Need a Wrongful Death Attorney

You're allowed to pursue a wrongful death claim without a lawyer, but families often face well-funded insurance carriers and defense teams whose goal is to minimize payout. An experienced attorney can protect your family from avoidable mistakes and pursue the full value of your loss.

Preserving Evidence Before It Disappears

Key evidence can vanish fast — surveillance video gets overwritten, vehicles are repaired or destroyed, cell phone records expire, and witnesses become harder to locate. A lawyer can send preservation letters, coordinate inspections, and work with investigators and experts to protect your case.

Identifying All Responsible Parties

Wrongful death cases are often more complex than they appear. Responsibility may extend beyond the obvious party to include employers, vehicle owners, contractors, property owners, manufacturers, and even government entities. Finding every liable party and every available policy is one of the biggest drivers of recovery.

Valuing the Claim Completely

Insurance companies may quickly offer a settlement that sounds helpful but fails to account for the long-term financial and household impact of the loss. A wrongful death lawyer will build a damages model supported by income records, benefits documentation, and testimony about the value of lost care and companionship.

Managing Deadlines and Legal Requirements

Most wrongful death lawsuits must be filed within two years of the date of death under California law. Some cases have even shorter deadlines — especially when a government agency may be involved. An attorney can spot these issues early and keep the case compliant.

Know Your Rights

California Wrongful Death Law: What You Need to Know

Wrongful death claims have their own rules about who may sue and what can be recovered. Here are the essentials.

Two-Year Filing Deadline

Under California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1, a wrongful death lawsuit generally must be filed within two years of the death. If a government entity is involved, a claim can be due in as little as six months.

Who Can File

California Code of Civil Procedure § 377.60 limits who may bring a wrongful death claim — typically the surviving spouse or domestic partner, children, and certain dependents. A related 'survival action' (§ 377.34) lets the estate pursue the losses the victim suffered before death.

Damages Available

Families can recover economic damages (lost financial support, funeral and burial costs) and non-economic damages (loss of love, companionship, and guidance). California's pure comparative negligence rule applies to reduce, not bar, recovery.

Common Questions

Wrongful Death FAQs

Who can file a wrongful death claim in California?

California Code of Civil Procedure § 377.60 limits who may sue, typically the surviving spouse or domestic partner, children, and certain financial dependents. If there is no surviving family in those categories, others who would inherit may be eligible.

How long do I have to file a wrongful death claim in California?

You generally have two years from the date of death to file a wrongful death lawsuit under California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1. If a government entity is involved, you may have as little as six months to file a claim, so it is important to speak with an attorney quickly.

What damages can my family recover in a wrongful death case?

Families can recover economic damages such as lost financial support and funeral and burial expenses, and non-economic damages such as the loss of the deceased's love, companionship, and guidance. A separate survival action may recover losses the victim suffered before death.
