What Does a Personal Injury Lawyer Do?

A personal injury lawyer provides legal support and guidance that can be invaluable after a serious accident. They handle critical tasks throughout your claim so you can focus on recovery.

While accident victims can pursue claims on their own, it is generally not recommended — especially if they are unfamiliar with the legal process. Without experienced representation, the chances of recovering a lower compensation amount increase significantly, as victims are more susceptible to tactics designed to minimize their payout.

  • Collect & preserve evidence — securing documentation before it's lost or destroyed
  • Negotiate with insurance companies — countering tactics designed to minimize your payout
  • Hold responsible parties accountable — fighting for your best interests every step of the way
Know Your Deadlines

Statute of Limitations on Personal Injury Cases

There is a strict deadline on all personal injury cases in California. Missing it can permanently waive your right to pursue compensation.

Standard Personal Injury Claims

2 Years

According to California Civil Code §335.1, all personal injury claims must be filed within two years from the date of the accident.

Claims Against Government Entities

6 Months

According to California Government Code §911.2, accident lawsuits involving a government entity must be filed within six months from the date of the accident.

Protect Your Rights

Steps to Take After an Accident

If you've been involved in an accident, taking the right steps immediately can protect your rights and strengthen your case.

1

Call 911

Have first responders take control of the situation, document the scene, and create an official accident report.

2

Document Everything

Take photos and videos of the accident scene and exchange contact and insurance information with everyone involved.

3

See a Doctor

Get medical attention to ensure you haven't suffered any serious injuries — some symptoms don't appear until days later.

4

Contact an Attorney

Reach out to a personal injury lawyer immediately so they can protect your rights and start building your case.

What You Can Recover

Types of Recoverable Compensation

In personal injury cases, victims can recover compensation for costs that have accrued due to the accident. Recoverable damages fall into three categories.

Economic Damages

Calculable expenses that have accrued due to the accident — designed to make you financially whole again.

  • Medical expenses
  • Lost wages
  • Loss of earning capacity
  • Property damages
  • Funeral/burial costs

Non-Economic Damages

Subjective costs that compensate victims for physical pain and emotional suffering caused by the accident.

  • Pain and suffering
  • Emotional distress
  • Loss of enjoyment of life
  • Loss of consortium

Punitive Damages

A rare form of damages that punishes negligent parties for their actions rather than compensating the victim directly.

  • Deters similar future behavior
  • Sets legal precedent
  • Holds responsible parties accountable

Note: Proper documentation is required to recover these expenses. An experienced attorney ensures all damages are thoroughly documented.
