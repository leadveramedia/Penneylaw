Understanding Your Rights

Common Carrier Liability in Bus Accidents

Buses are considered "common carriers" under California law, which means they are held to a higher standard of care than ordinary drivers. Bus companies and operators have a duty to exercise the utmost care and diligence for the safety of their passengers.

This heightened duty of care means that bus companies can be held liable for injuries caused by negligent driving, inadequate vehicle maintenance, failure to properly screen and train drivers, and other safety violations. When a bus company fails to meet this standard, injured passengers may be entitled to substantial compensation.

Additionally, claims against government-operated buses (such as city transit systems) have special rules and strict filing deadlines. In California, you typically have only six months to file a claim against a government entity, making it crucial to contact an attorney as soon as possible after an accident.

Potential Liable Parties

  • Bus driver (for negligent operation)
  • Bus company or transit authority
  • Government agencies (city, county, state)
  • Maintenance companies
  • Bus or parts manufacturers
  • Other negligent drivers

Accident Causes

Common Causes of Bus Accidents

Bus collisions can happen for many of the same reasons as car crashes, but buses add more complexity because of their size, routes, and schedules.

Driver-Related Factors

  • Fatigue from long shifts or split schedules
  • Distracted driving (phone, dispatch radio, passenger issues)
  • Speeding or unsafe lane changes
  • Inadequate training for route hazards, weather, or night driving

Company or Agency Negligence

  • Hiring and retention problems (poor safety record, insufficient screening)
  • Unrealistic schedules that encourage rushing
  • Failure to enforce safety rules (seatbelt policies, passenger limits, safe stops)

Maintenance and Mechanical Issues

  • Brake or tire failure
  • Steering and suspension problems
  • Worn lights, mirrors, or safety equipment
  • Skipped inspections or incomplete repairs

Roadway and Third-Party Causes

  • Dangerous intersections, poor visibility, or missing signage
  • Poorly designed bus stops or unsafe pull-outs
  • Another driver cutting off the bus or running a red light
  • Pedestrian and cyclist conflicts near bus lanes and stops
Severe Injuries

How Serious Are Bus Accidents?

Bus accidents tend to cause more severe injuries than many passenger-vehicle crashes because buses generate more momentum and impact force, take longer to stop, and can throw passengers around due to a lack of seatbelts.

Even if you believe you have suffered minor injuries, it is important to see a medical professional as soon as possible. Symptoms can worsen over time and, depending on the condition, can be fatal. Getting medical care quickly also creates documentation that can be crucial to your claim.
