Common Personal Injury Cases We Handle

Our firm represents clients across Redding and the surrounding communities. Below are some of the most common types of cases we see in the region.

Car Accidents

Redding's busy roadways — Interstate 5, Highway 44, and North Market Street — see frequent collisions caused by distracted or impaired drivers. We handle rear-end crashes, T-bone collisions, and multi-vehicle pileups, pursuing compensation for your medical bills and lost income.

Truck Accidents

Redding sits along major freight routes between Oregon and Central California, making heavy truck traffic a serious risk. Whether it's an 18-wheeler accident or commercial vehicle crash, our attorneys work with accident reconstruction experts to prove liability.

Motorcycle Accidents

Scenic routes like Placer Street and Eureka Way attract motorcyclists, but careless drivers often fail to share the road. We work to recover compensation for injuries, bike repairs, and long-term care.

Slip and Fall Accidents

Property owners in Redding — whether a business in the Mt. Shasta Mall, a restaurant on Market Street, or an apartment complex near Lake Boulevard — must maintain safe premises. We pursue claims for injuries caused by wet floors, uneven sidewalks, poor lighting, and other hazards.

Dog Bites

California's strict liability law (Civil Code 3342) means dog owners are responsible for bites, even if the dog has never shown aggression before. Whether the attack happened in Caldwell Park or a private residence, we'll pursue damages for medical treatment, scarring, and emotional trauma.

Wrongful Death

If you've lost a loved one because of another person's negligence, our firm will fight to hold the responsible party accountable. We pursue compensation for funeral costs, loss of income, and the immeasurable loss of companionship.

Workplace Injuries

From industrial worksites to retail stores, Redding workers face daily risks. We handle workers' compensation claims and third-party personal injury lawsuits for those injured due to unsafe conditions, defective machinery, or negligent contractors.

Time Limits

How Long Do You Have to File a Claim

Under California Code of Civil Procedure § 335.1, you have two years from the date of your injury to file a personal injury lawsuit. However, for claims that involve government entities, accident victims will only have 6 months to file a claim as per California Government Code 911.2.

Redding injury cases often involve complex factors such as multiple drivers, out-of-state trucking companies, or city-maintained roads. It's important to speak with an attorney immediately. Our team will determine the correct filing deadline and preserve critical evidence such as surveillance footage and accident reports from the Redding Police Department.
